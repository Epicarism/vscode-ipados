- **Implementation:** Defined in `VSCodeiPadOSApp.swift` line 148 as `.keyboardShortcut("b", modifiers: .command)` in `ViewMenuCommands`. Posts `NSNotification.Name("ToggleSidebar")` → received in `ContentView.swift` line 135-137 → calls `editorCore.toggleSidebar()` (EditorCore.swift lines 990-994: `withAnimation(.spring(response: 0.3)) { showSidebar.toggle() }`)
- **Root Cause:** The Runestone `TextView` (a UITextView subclass, defined in `RunestoneEditorView.swift`) intercepts Cmd+B as the system "Bold" text formatting command. UITextView has built-in key command handlers for Cmd+B/I/U that take priority over SwiftUI's `.commands` modifier. Even when the text view doesn't have explicit first responder status, the responder chain appears to consume the shortcut before it reaches the menu command system.

## Recommended Fix for Cmd+B
The Runestone `TextView` (UITextView subclass) needs to override `canPerformAction(_:withSender:)` or provide custom `keyCommands` to prevent Cmd+B from being intercepted as "Bold". Alternatively, the app could use a UIKeyCommand at the UIViewController level in the responder chain to catch Cmd+B before UITextView does.
